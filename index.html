Bio

My current research focuses on the mathematical and algorithmic foundations of AI, especially diffusion models, flow-based generative models, reinforcement learning, and the intersection between reinforcement learning and generative AI. I am interested in how AI systems can become not only expressive and scalable, but also reliable, physically consistent, and capable of robust behavior under distributional shifts.

My interest in generative models was motivated by the striking progress of text-to-image and text-to-video systems, as well as their remaining limitations. Although modern models can generate high-resolution and visually realistic content, they may still violate physical constraints, temporal consistency, and semantic plausibility. I view these failures as a form of hallucination and am interested in developing more reliable generative mechanisms.

I also believe that supervised learning and passive perception alone are insufficient for the path toward more general intelligence. Interaction, experience acquisition, and feedback are essential, which makes reinforcement learning a key component of next-generation AI. I am currently exploring both foundational methods and possible applications, including embodied intelligence and trustworthy AI.
